About Us

Founder and Chief Instructor, of the School Of Survival (SOS) in Kodiak, Alaska, Brendan (Ben) Dent brings his larger than life personality while making it fun and entertaining to learn.  

He has lived in many states throughout his Coast Guard career, but calls Alaska his home, and lives here with his wife and 2 dogs.

 Ben is often found fishing for salmon when he's not working or exploring new hunting grounds.

 Having lived in Oregon, California, Michigan, and now Alaska, he enjoys everything the outdoors have to offer.  

Ben's background include emergency medical and survival training provided by the nation’s top trainers. He has personally rescued survivors of plane crashes, and kayakers who got themselves in over their heads.

Come attend a class, or two, with SOS and hear what most instructors avoid talking about and makes these sessions unique.

Only Ben can share his unique perspective on surviving when it seems all hope is lost.

About your instructor

Ben hikes into the backcoutry where roads are few and deer and elk are plentiful.  He takes his boat out fishing in the coastal waters of the Bering Sea to catch salmon, crab, and halibut. 

We ❤ hiking, fishing, and the outdoor life

To be honest, it's not all sunshine and sight-seeing.  Once you leave the paved road, your chances of injury, or getting lost, go up dramatically.  Imagine discovering yourself turned around, or possibly injured and the battery on your phone is dead...  

  • Can you get yourself rescued?  (If you're lost, how to reorient yourself?)
  • Can you keep a level head, long enough to make it back home?
  • What is your risk of hypothermia and how will you combat it when it's pouring rain and you have no matches?

We love the outdoors, but add some rugged terrain, some adverse weather, bears or other predators, and the odds of surviving begin to drop.

Come learn how to realistically assess your situation in any moment, correctly prioritize the tasks that you will need to perform, and plan for a potential rescue.  Learn shelter building from found or natural materials, learn to collect and ration drinking water, learn how to do the next right thing to get back to your family and friends.  

(If you think you can afford to wait for a plane to fly over, or a friend to notice that you're missing, you probably need this class.)

Understand your own abilities and limits, and maximize your opportunities both in terms of being seen, and living another day.
